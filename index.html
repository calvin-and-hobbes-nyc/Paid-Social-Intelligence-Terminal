Meta
Measurement change

Meta narrows click-through attribution and forces teams to reset the story they tell.

Meta is redefining click-through attribution for website and in-store conversions around actual link clicks, while moving social interactions like likes, shares, and saves into engage-through views.

This is a reporting and client-education story as much as a platform story. Historical comparisons may look weaker even when underlying performance is stable.

Recommended strategy Re-baseline benchmarks, distinguish click and engagement narratives clearly, and pair platform reporting with stronger business framing when needed.

TikTok
Commerce + automation

TikTok is becoming more operationally integrated across paid, creator, commerce, and profile governance.

Its recent product roadmap expands Smart+, Market Scope, and creator workflows, while profile-based identity changes make owned presence and verification more important to campaign readiness.

The implication is bigger than a feature launch: teams now need tighter alignment between paid social, organic social, creator management, and brand governance.

Recommended strategy Keep an automation testing lane, but treat profile health, verification, and content operations as part of your media infrastructure.

Platform Briefings

Additional platform notes, headlines, and recommended moves.

LinkedIn
Company-level attribution strengthens the B2B story.

LinkedIn’s Company Intelligence API gives marketers a better way to discuss account-level influence and buying-group engagement, rather than relying only on lead counts.

Pinterest
Top of Search pushes Pinterest further into demand capture.

The platform is getting more useful for lower-funnel, high-intent moments, especially when paired with clean shopping feeds and better catalog readiness.

Reddit
Reddit automation expands, but community context still matters.

Max campaigns make scaling easier, but operators should still test whether community-native planning outperforms automation in nuanced categories.

X
Vertical video continues to define X’s ad story.

X is easier to position as a fast-moving contextual video extension, especially when creative can be reused from other vertical video ecosystems.

Snapchat
Sponsored Snaps make Snap more defensible in performance planning.

Snap’s more direct-response positioning means it belongs in more app and commerce testing plans than it did a few years ago.

YouTube / Google Ads
Demand Gen and Shorts keep looking more like paid social.

As the surfaces become more visual, native, and commerce-oriented, planning teams should integrate them more tightly with broader social and creator strategy.
